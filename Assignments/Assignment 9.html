    • Week 12 - Arduino III

    Exercise 17

    For exercise 17, we connected a matrix keypad to an arduino, and programmed the Matrix Keypad for keying information, with the program used using arrays and the keypad library alongside other programming techniques.

    Exercise 18

    For exercise 18, we connected an LDR, LED and a resistor to an arduino. Since the voltage across an LDR changes as the surrounding light intensity changes, code was written to allow the LDR to control the LED, such that the LED lights up once the detected value produced by the LDR becomes greater than 200 and turns off if the value goes below 200.

    Exercise 19

    For exercise 19, we connected an ultrasonic sensor, and LED and a resistor to make a circuit that turns on the LED if the ultrasonic sensor detects something at within a certain distance of itself. The arduino was also coded to print the distance detetcted by the ultrasonic sensor to the serial monitor.

    Exercise 20

    For exercise 20, we connected a PIR motion sensor to an arduino. We then wrote code that printed the number of times motion has been detected by the sensor to the serial monitor, with there being a 2 second delay every time motion was detected.

    Exercise 21

    For exercise 21, we connected a temperature sensor, 3 LEDs and 3 resistors, before writing a program that displays the temperature from the temperature sensor in the serial monitor and turns on the LEDs in sequence as the temperature detected increases.

    Exercise 22

    For exercise 22, we connected a potentiometer, a resistor and an LED to an arduino, and programmed the LED to increase in brightness as the potentiometer is turned clockwise.

    Exercise 23

    For exercise 23, we connected an RGB LED and 3 resistors to an arduino, and programmed the LED to swap between random colours through randomizing the red, green and blue input values of the LED every 500ms.

    Exercise 24

    For exercise 24, we connected 2 neopixel strips to a external power supply and an arduino, and used code that flashes all LEDs red, green then blue, before producing running lights in randomized colours then looping back to flashing all LEDs red, green then blue.

    Exercise 25

    For exercise 25, we connected an external power supply, motor, transistor, potentiometer and resistor to an arduino, and programmed it such that the speed of the motor is controlled by the potentiometer, with the motor increasing speed as the potentiometer is turned further clockwise.

    Exercise 26

    For exercise 26, we connected a stepper motor (motor with encoder in tinkercad) and arduino together to program the motor to spin at different speeds for 2038 steps every 1000ms on loop.

    Exercise 27

    For exercise 27, we connected a positional servo motor, external power supply and arduino together, and programmed the servo motor to turn anti-clockwise in increments of 10 degrees from 0 degrees to 110 degrees before looping back to its original position. The angle at which the servo motor was turning to was also printed to the serial monitor every time the servo motor turned.

    Exercise 28

    For exercise 28, we connected a continuous servo motor, external power supply and an arduino together, and programmed the servo motor to spin continuously in the direction specified by the degree the potentiometer is turned to, with it spinning clockwise when the potentiometer is at an angle below 93 degrees and anti-clockwise when the angle is above 93 degrees. The speed of the motor also varies with the degree of the potentiometer, with the speed being the slowest at an angle of 93 degrees and fastest in each direction at 180 degrees and 0 degrees respectively. The output value of the potentiometer and the corresponding angle value are also printed to the serial monitor when the potentiometer is turned.

    Exercise 29

    For exercise 29, we connected a LCD display and an arduino, and wrote a program to display the text "Hello World!" on the top row and "LCD Tutorial" on the bottom row of the LCD display, requiring the inclusion of the I2C LCD display library.

    Exercise 30

    For exercise 30, we connected a piezo and an arduino, and used code that plays a two tone alarm on the piezo.
